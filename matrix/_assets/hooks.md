# Hook Library

> High-performing hook structures, adaptable across clients.
> Reference during concept generation in Step 3 of the matrix build workflow.

Hooks must pass the Andromeda test: front-loaded with plain-language
keywords the target audience uses in their own language. First 2-5 seconds
decide whether the algorithm promotes the content.

---

## Question Hooks

Most GEO-friendly. Match the exact phrasing of searches.

- "How much does {{X}} actually cost?"
- "Is {{X}} worth it in 2026?"
- "What does {{X}} look like when it goes wrong?"
- "Should you {{A}} or {{B}}?"
- "How long does {{X}} really take?"

## Contrast Hooks

Force attention through opposition.

- "Everyone says {{X}}. Here's why {{opposite}}."
- "I used to believe {{X}}. Now I think {{Y}}."
- "The {{industry}} tells you {{X}}. The truth is {{Y}}."

## Cost Reveal Hooks

Pattern-break through transparency.

- "Here's what {{X}} actually costs in 2026. No quotes. No calls. Just numbers."
- "I just sent this invoice. Here's the breakdown."
- "This cost {{$X}}. Was it worth it?"

## Story Hooks (SAO)

For success stories. Situation > Action > Outcome.

- "A year ago, {{client}} had {{problem}}. Today they have {{outcome}}. Here's what we did."
- "{{Client}} came to us with {{situation}}. We did {{action}}. Six months later, {{outcome}}."

## Pain Acknowledgement Hooks

Name the fear the audience hasn't said out loud.

- "You're probably worried that {{fear}}. You're right to worry. Here's why and what to do."
- "If you've ever {{pain}}, this is for you."
- "The thing nobody tells you about {{X}}..."

## Meme / Reaction Hooks

For lower-funnel, reach-oriented content.

- "POV: you {{relatable situation in audience's life}}."
- "Me: {{expectation}}. Also me: {{reality}}."
- "When {{scenario}} and you {{reaction}}."

## Authority Hooks

For Talking Head / long-form.

- "After {{specific experience number}}, here's the {{lesson}} I wish I'd known."
- "I've {{credibility}}. Here's the {{number}} things I'd do differently."
- "{{N}} things I've learned {{doing X}} that changed how I think about {{Y}}."

## Curiosity Gap Hooks

Force the scroll stop without clickbait.

- "This is the thing most {{audience}} get wrong about {{X}}."
- "There's a reason {{observation}}. It's not what you'd expect."
- "{{Number}} {{things}} that {{surprising claim}}."

---

## Hooks That Failed

Anti-patterns. Don't repeat.

- *"Have you ever wondered..."* → too slow, algorithm skips
- *"In this video I'm going to..."* → meta, no substance in first 3 seconds
- *"So basically what happened was..."* → buries the hook
- *"Guys, I'm so excited to..."* → audience performative filler
