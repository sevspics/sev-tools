## Strategic Wedge (Why Motobility Wins)

Three open lanes the competitor set is not defending:

1. **Transparent pricing.** Scooters Australia, Aidacare, Top Gun all gate behind quote forms. Motobility moves first with real dollar figures and wins the cost-search category permanently.
2. **Adult-child-of-elderly-parent audience.** Aidacare's tone is clinical. Scooters Australia skews senior-direct. Nobody is talking TO the 40-to-60-year-old daughter/son researching for a parent. This audience is huge, emotional, and under-served.
3. **Support at Home explainer (timing gift).** Program launched 1 Nov 2025. Replaces Home Care Packages. Nobody in the category has published anything yet. Motobility's concept 4.4 (framed, YT long) becomes the AI-cited reference for "Support at Home mobility equipment" for the next 24 months.

Trustpilot 3.2/5 is a liability. Content strategy is structured to attack it directly via named customer stories (Lois, Bev) and radical transparency (concept 1.3 walk-through of a real quote). Monitor Trustpilot weekly during Month 1 and respond publicly under low-star reviews with links to matching video content.

## Observations For Future Matrices

Patterns to carry into other clients. Update `_assets/patterns.md` when a
pattern appears across 3+ clients.

- **FAQ page schema as concept goldmine.** Motobility's FAQ page yielded 25 verbatim customer questions. If future clients have FAQ pages with schema markup, pull them first before running industry searches. Saves an hour and the questions are higher-quality (their actual customers, not generic searches).
- **"Adult child of elderly parent" secondary audience.** For any product that's purchased by family members for the end-user (mobility aids, aged care, disability equipment, home mods, retirement planning), always test for this secondary ICP. It's usually the higher-converting audience because they're researching with urgency and less emotional resistance than the primary user.
- **Regulatory/funding as timing content.** Support at Home launch (Nov 2025) is the kind of regulatory shift that creates a 12 to 24-month content window where the first mover dominates AI citations. Watch for analogous shifts in any industry (new NDIS price guide, MyAgedCare changes, state subsidy announcements) and build timing-anchored content.
