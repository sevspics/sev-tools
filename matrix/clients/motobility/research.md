### Top 5 Industry Pain Points (customer language)
1. **"My scooter broke and no one came."** Dealer ghosting post-sale is the category's dirtiest secret. Lifestyle Mobility went into receivership. Buyers feel stranded.
2. **"I bought the wrong one for Mum."** Adult children picking 4 km/h scooters for parents who walk faster than that. Non-locking handlebars. Wrong size for the house.
3. **"The battery is dead again."** 80% of mobility-scooter breakdowns are battery-related. Buyers don't know lead-acid lasts 1 to 2 years vs lithium 4 to 7.
4. **"Will it fit my house, my car, my stairs?"** Indoor manoeuvring, narrow hallways, car boot, airline 300Wh limit, bus access.
5. **"Am I being ripped off by NDIS?"** 6-month AT approval waits, OT report requirements, fear of buying privately and losing eligibility.

### Misconceptions & Objections
- "Using a scooter means I'm giving up." Studies show over-reliance correlates with BMI rise and muscle decline. Content angle: how to stay active WITH a scooter.
- "NDIS will pay for anything I choose." Reality: level 3/4 AT needs OT assessment, only "reasonable and necessary" covered.
- "All scooters are the same." Indoor vs outdoor, 3-wheel vs 4-wheel, battery and range decide everything.
- "I need a licence." Generally no, except QLD registration rules.
- "Lithium is dangerous." Reputable LiFePO4 is stable. NSW introduced mandatory safety standards Feb 2025.

### Emerging Trends (2025 / 2026)
- **Support at Home** (replaced Home Care Packages 1 Nov 2025). Can fund scooters, home mods. OTs and consumers are currently navigating the new system. Huge content opportunity because the program literally just changed.
- NSW lithium safety standards (effective 1 Feb 2025) spreading to other states.
- Airline-approved 300Wh folding scooters. Cruise and travel boom among retired Australians.
- AT assessor shortages. Longer waits pushing buyers toward private purchase pathways.
- AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AIO) increasingly used by adult children researching for parents. GEO-ready content is massively underpriced.

### Regulatory / Funding Context (all directly relevant)
- **NDIS:** Registered provider. Ages 7 to 65. Level 3/4 AT assessment. Up to 6-month approval.
- **DVA RAP (Rehabilitation Appliances Program):** Gold Card full access, White Card conditional. Covers scooters, wheelchairs, lift chairs, adjustable beds. GP/specialist initiates.
- **Support at Home:** New as of 1 Nov 2025. Replaces Home Care Packages. Can fund scooters and home mods.
- **CHSP** and **$25M GEAT top-up**: equipment and assistive tech for seniors at home.
- **State rules:** Under 10 km/h = pedestrian status. QLD requires registration. WA-specific public-transport rules (Motobility already has a PDF on this).

## Competitor Analysis

### Competitor 1: Scooters Australia
- **URL:** scootersaus.com.au
- **Content output:** Strong blog (cost guides, troubleshooting, case studies like Sam and carer, Meg Wade horse-riding champion). Publishing regularly.
- **Gaps:** Little DVA-specific content. Scooter-focused, light on stairlifts and chairs. Weak east-coast SEO.
- **Price positioning:** Same mid-market band.

### Competitor 2: Aidacare
- **URL:** aidacare.com.au
- **Content output:** Nationwide, 30+ years. Active YouTube (@aidacareYT) with product demos, expo content from ATSA Sydney 2024, user testimonials. Publishes clinical "Product Application Guides" by condition (stroke, arthritis, dementia, falls).
- **Gaps:** Tone is clinical and institutional. No adult-child-researching-for-parent content. No warmth or relatable storytelling.
- **Price positioning:** Similar mid-market, institutional-feeling.

### Competitor 3: Top Gun Mobility
- **URL:** topgunmobility.com.au
- **Content output:** Essentially zero. Catalogue-only site. No blog, no video, no education.
- **Gaps:** No funding guidance visible to end consumers. No content layer at all. Dealer-network model so consumer experience is thin.
- **Price positioning:** Same mid-market band.

### Category-Wide Gap
> **Nobody publishes transparent pricing.** Every competitor gates behind quote forms. **Nobody speaks to the adult-child-of-elderly-parent buyer** directly. **Nobody is currently explaining Support at Home (launched Nov 2025).** These three gaps are Motobility's open lanes.
