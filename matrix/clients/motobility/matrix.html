What Your Audience Is Already Asking

01 · Research

Top Pain Points

  • "My scooter broke and no one came." Dealer ghosting is the category's dirtiest secret. Lifestyle Mobility went into receivership. Buyers feel stranded.
  • "I bought the wrong one for Mum." Adult children pick 4 km/h scooters for parents who walk faster. Wrong size for the house.
  • "The battery is dead again." 80% of breakdowns are battery-related. Buyers don't know lead-acid lasts 1 to 2 years vs lithium 4 to 7.
  • "Will it fit my house, my car, my stairs?" Narrow hallways, car boot, airline 300 Wh limit, bus access.
  • "Am I being ripped off by NDIS?" 6-month AT approval waits, OT report requirements, fear of losing eligibility.

Questions Your Audience Is Searching

  • "How much does a mobility scooter cost in Australia?"
  • "Can I get a mobility scooter on NDIS, aged care package, or DVA?"
  • "Mobility scooter vs electric wheelchair. Which one?"
  • "Is a mobility scooter actually worth it?"
  • "Do I need a licence to ride a mobility scooter in Australia?"
  • "Are mobility scooters legal on footpaths, roads, and buses?"
  • "Lithium vs lead-acid battery. Which lasts longer?"
  • "My mobility scooter won't turn on. What now?"
  • "How much does a stairlift cost installed?"
  • "What is Support at Home and does it cover mobility equipment?"

Research Methodology

  • Pages fetched: homepage, /faqs, /why-us, /information-about-ndis, /grants, /contact-us, /testimonials, /blog, scooter category + sub-pages.
  • Searches run: 10+ queries across cost, funding, comparisons, pain points, best-of, and local (Perth + Mornington).
  • Competitors analysed: Scooters Australia, Aidacare, Top Gun Mobility.
  • FAQ page extraction: 25 verbatim customer questions pulled from FAQ schema. Every one is a content prompt.
  • Trust signal flag: Trustpilot 3.2/5 ("Average"). Content strategy attacks this deficit directly via named customer stories, transparent pricing, and behind-the-scenes service content.
  • Timing gift: Support at Home launched 1 Nov 2025. Nobody in the category has explained it yet. Motobility moves first.
  • Geographic hook: Mornington VIC store (July 2025) anchors east-coast content expansion. Week 4 of calendar leads with it.

Your 6 Pillars, Your Angle

02 · Pillars
Pillar 01

Costs

Nobody in Australian mobility equipment publishes real prices. We will. Stairlifts, scooters, lift chairs, each with dollar figures and the reasons behind them. Transparency is the untouched lane.

Pillar 02

Pain Points

Adult children buying for elderly parents are scared and under-informed. We name the fears (wrong scooter, warranty ghosting, NDIS maze, battery failure) and solve them before the phone call.

Pillar 03

Success Stories

Named customers, real transformations. Lois, Bev, Brendan. Before and after. Never generic, always specific. Every story has a first name and a suburb.

Pillar 04

Comparisons

Lithium vs lead-acid. Stannah vs OtoLift. NDIS vs DVA vs Support at Home. 3-wheel vs 4-wheel. We compare honestly, including where we don't win. Law of the Coin earns the trust.

Pillar 05

Best-of Lists

Airline-approved scooters. Curved stairlifts ranked. Mornington Peninsula accessible destinations. We rank, so we become the authority instead of another option in the search results.

Pillar 06

Practitioner Journey

"You've got a friend" is the tagline. We film what that actually means: mobile technicians, multilingual staff, 25 years in Perth, the new Mornington store. The humanisation layer that Aidacare can't touch.

  • Shoot day 1: All Talking Head pieces (6 concepts + Selling 7 videos 01, 02, 03, 07). Studio setup at Osborne Park. Teleprompter. Founder + senior salesperson. One full day.
  • Shoot day 2: Framed documentary pieces. On-location at Bev's home (concept 3.4 / S7#5), at a stairlift install, at the Mornington store (concept 5.4).
  • Shoot day 3: B-roll + Faceless content. Warehouse, service bay, mobile technician vehicle, product close-ups (batteries, lift chairs, stairlift rails).
  • Candid content: Can be captured ad-hoc by staff with phones. Weekly check-in to review and edit down the best clips.
  • Animation pipeline: Visual Storyteller concepts (p1-visual, p2-visual, p3-visual, p4-visual, p5-visual, p6-visual) are After Effects or Cavalry. Outsource to motion designer. Batch in two rounds.
  • Memes: Cheap, fast, can be produced in-house by social lead. Don't overthink. Ship.
  • Customer approvals: Every named customer (Lois, Bev, named unboxings) needs signed release before posting.
  • Trustpilot counter-strategy: After first 30 days, cross-post top short-form winners as responses under Trustpilot reviews to pull the rating up organically.
  • Mornington launch: Week 4 anchors around the new VIC store. Treat as a soft-launch media push. Repurpose 5.4 across paid IG/FB targeted to Mornington Peninsula postcodes.
