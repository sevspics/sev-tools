# Content Matrix Methodology Reference

The canonical explanation of the 6x6 Content Matrix and its integration with
the Endless Customers Selling 7, for use inside the content-matrix-builder
skill. This methodology was established in Sev's Session 1 advisory with
Daecian Sparkes-Young and refined through Sessions 2-7.

---

## The Core Principle

One attempt is not a test. The matrix exists to force volume across distinct
angles and executions so the creator can actually identify what works, rather
than guessing from a sample size of 1.

6 pillars x 6 formats = 36 concepts minimum per client.
The standard 1:1 assignment: pick 3 ideas, produce 10 of each = 30 pieces
before drawing any conclusions.

---

## The 6 Pillars (Topics)

These map loosely to Marcus Sheridan's Big 5 (Endless Customers) but with
two Sev-specific additions (Best-of Lists split out, Practitioner Journey
added for creator/service brands).

### 1. Costs
Pricing transparency. "How much does X cost?" The category everyone searches
for and almost no business answers honestly. Also covers: why prices vary,
cost breakdowns, why cheap is risky, ROI framing, what a fair price looks
like, hidden costs buyers don't anticipate.

### 2. Pain Points
The problems the audience actually has. Warning signs, what goes wrong, what
people regret, how to avoid pitfalls. Speaks to the buyer BEFORE they're
ready to buy. Builds trust by naming the fear they haven't said out loud yet.

### 3. Success Stories
Case studies, transformations, before-and-afters, client wins with numbers.
The trust engine. Uses the SAO framework: Situation, Action, Outcome.
Jake Standard / Hero's Journey structure on longer format.

### 4. Comparisons
Them vs competitors, product A vs product B, DIY vs professional, premium vs
budget, in-house vs outsource. Buyers search for comparisons constantly.
Honest comparisons (including admitting when the competitor is better on
something) build disproportionate trust.

### 5. Best-of Lists
Best [service] in [location], top 5 features to look for, best tools,
expert picks. Pure authority positioning. Ranks the creator as the arbiter,
not a participant.

### 6. Practitioner Journey
Behind the scenes, day-in-the-life, founder story, team introductions,
process reveals. The humanisation layer. Counters the "course bro" / 
"faceless corporation" perception that kills trust with modern audiences.

## The 6 Formats (Executions)

Every pillar gets produced in every format across a full content cycle.

### 1. Faceless
No on-camera talent. Voiceover + B-roll, screen recordings, text-on-screen,
stock footage or shot footage with no presenter. Perfect for founders who
hate being on camera, or for process-heavy/product-focused content.

### 2. Memes
Text-based humour. Relatable punchlines, visual gags, reaction-style edits.
Mass reach vehicle. Top-of-funnel discovery. The "9.5k view meme" benchmark
Daecian set in Session 2 is the reference point.

### 3. Candid
Phone-shot, raw, unpolished. Behind-the-scenes feel. The opposite of
performative. Builds humanness and "they're a real person" trust.

### 4. Framed
Intentional B-roll, shot-listed, edited with style. Documentary quality.
Medium effort to produce but high trust payoff. Shows craft and care.
Usually shot on proper camera with attention to lighting.

### 5. Visual Storyteller
Animation, diagrams, kinetic typography, illustrated explainers. Cuts
through complex concepts. Also differentiates visually in an infinite scroll
feed. Premium-feeling and authority-building.

### 6. Talking Head
Direct-to-camera, either as a standalone piece or a podcast clip. The
authority delivery format. Non-negotiable for YouTube long-form and GEO.
Also the format the Selling 7 mostly live in.

## The 6x6 Matrix

|  | Faceless | Memes | Candid | Framed | Visual | Talking |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Costs** | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |
| **Pain Points** | 7 | 8 | 9 | 10 | 11 | 12 |
| **Success Stories** | 13 | 14 | 15 | 16 | 17 | 18 |
| **Comparisons** | 19 | 20 | 21 | 22 | 23 | 24 |
| **Best-of Lists** | 25 | 26 | 27 | 28 | 29 | 30 |
| **Practitioner Journey** | 31 | 32 | 33 | 34 | 35 | 36 |

Each cell is a distinct concept. The constraint is what forces creative
thinking. If a cell feels awkward ("Costs as a meme?") the resulting
concept is usually the most viral one in the matrix because nobody else
in the category is producing anything like it.

## The 80/20 Rule

In execution, 80% of content should be proven formats and angles, 20%
experimental. The matrix supplies the inventory. What you run the first
time is 80% grounded in what the research says the audience is asking for,
and 20% is testing adjacent or wild angles.

## Distribution Math

Worst-case framework:
```
10 videos per month
→ 10,000 total views (1,000 per video minimum is conservative on modern algos)
→ 10% engagement rate (1,000 engaged viewers)
→ 10% of engaged inquire (100 inquiries)
→ 10% conversion (10 clients)
```

Even at worst-case assumptions the math works. The client isn't being asked
to gamble. They're being asked to show up with volume and let percentages do
their job.

Scale for higher-ticket B2B: inquiry rate goes down, conversion rate goes up.
Scale for lower-ticket B2C: volume goes up, percentages wash out to similar
end result.

## The Selling 7 Overlay (GEO Layer)

These seven videos run PARALLEL to the matrix. They are not part of the 36.
They are the non-negotiable authority stack that lives on YouTube and is
embedded on the client's website. They are the assets that ChatGPT,
Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews will cite when answering questions about
the client's industry.

1. **80% Video** — Top 10 prospect questions answered in one long-form video
2. **Cost & Price Video** — The honest money conversation
3. **Product/Service-Fit** — Who it's for AND who it isn't for
4. **Landing Page Videos** — 60-90 second conversion assets per key page
5. **Customer Journey** — The Jake Standard case study format
6. **Bio / Founder Video** — The humanity anchor on the About page
7. **Claims We Make** — Proof videos for every bold marketing claim

GEO optimisation for titles:
- Plain language, not jargon
- Front-load the search query form
- Examples:
  - "How Much Does [Service] Cost in [Year]?"
  - "Is [Service] Worth It? Honest Review"
  - "[Product A] vs [Product B]: Which Is Better?"
  - "[Service]: Who It's For And Who It Isn't"

## How The Matrix + Selling 7 Work Together

The Selling 7 is the authority foundation — always produced, always on
YouTube, always embedded on the site.

The Matrix is the ongoing content engine — produced in volume, distributed
across short-form platforms, driving attention back to the authority stack.

Short-form matrix concepts drive traffic to:
- The client's profile (follow)
- The client's YouTube channel (longer watch)
- The client's website (where the Selling 7 live as trust assets)
- Inquiry/booking form (conversion)

A healthy month looks like:
- 15-20 short-form matrix posts
- 2-4 YouTube long-form uploads (matrix expansions + Selling 7)
- All 7 Selling 7 videos produced and embedded within the first 90 days

## Key References For Concept Generation

**The Andromeda rule (Daecian Session 3):** Instagram's algorithm scrapes
the first 2-5 seconds for relevance keywords. Hooks must front-load plain
language keywords the target audience actually searches for. No jargon
unless the audience speaks it.

**The SAO framework (Daecian Session 2):** All success story content
structured as Situation > Action > Outcome.

**The QQPP method (Endless Customers):** Open by asking the Question your
audience is asking, validate it's a Quality question, explain why it
matters, then deliver the Payoff.

**Law of the Coin (Endless Customers):** For every pro, name a con.
Admitting weaknesses makes the strengths more credible.

**Disarmament (Endless Customers):** Open controversial or sales-adjacent
content by naming the resistance the viewer is feeling. "You're probably
skeptical of this because..."

## What "Good" Looks Like

When the 36 concepts are laid out, a good matrix:
- Has zero generic concepts (everything references this specific client)
- Has zero repeat angles (36 distinct ideas, not 6 ideas dressed 6 ways)
- Has hooks that someone in the target audience would actually type into
  a search bar or stop scrolling for
- Makes the awkward combinations (Costs as Memes, Practitioner Journey as
  Visual Storyteller) into standout concepts rather than dodges
- Is executable by the client in a single batched shoot day plus follow-up
  editing — the production complexity is realistic for their team size
